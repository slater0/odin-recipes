Bo Luc Lac (Shaking Beef)

Description

This Vietnamese steak salad is served on a bed of greens and tomatoes, topped with pickled onions and a lime dipping sauce. It is a widely popular Asian dish that blends taste sensations to creates a harmony of flavor with every bite. The "shaking" in the name refers to the tossing of the beef back and forth in the wok after it's quickly seared.

Ingredients


For the Marinade
  • 2 pounds boneless beef sirloin, fillet, or rib eye, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 10 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Maggi seasoning or soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

For the Salad
  • 1 medium red onion, cut into paper-thin strips
  • 7 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar or cider vinegar
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 head bibb or Boston lettuce, cored, leaves torn into large pieces
  • 1 cup shaved fennel bulb
  • 1 cup baby arugula
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 6 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the cubed beef, garlic, Maggi or soy sauce, sugar, salt and black pepper to taste. Let stand at room temperature for 1 hour.

  2. Prepare 4 plates with a bed of lettuce, onions, and tomotoes.

  3. Preheat a large skillet over high heat and add vegetable oil. Add remaining 5 cloves minced garlic. Add half the beef cubes and sear on all sides, shaking pan, until medium rare, about 1 minute. Reserve, then repeat with remaining beef.

  4. Transfer beef to salad greens and toss, or serve salad and beef separately, divided among four plates. Sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper. If desired, serve with rice or crusty French bread.

  5. Create dipping sauce by mixing lime, salt, and pepper to taste.
